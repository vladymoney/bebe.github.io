The benefits of swimming

The benefits of swimming and water are immeasurable. Little swimmers get sick less often because the water strengthens their overall health and thus boosts immunity. Swimming babies become better learning children, they are distinguished by rare balance and the ability to make correct decisions in difficult situations. They are more likely to learn complex motor skills and are more confident interacting with other children and adults as they grow.

Swimming develops the baby's respiratory system. The pressure of the water on the chest increases the depth of exhalation, and this leads to a deeper inhalation. This is how the child learns to breathe properly. These deep inhalations and exhalations "ventilate" the lungs and expel the bacteria.Thus, swimming becomes a powerful prevention against diseases of the upper respiratory tract. The physical exercises that the child performs in water from early childhood contribute to increasing lung capacity. That is why swimming is extremely suitable for children suffering from bronchopulmonary dysplasia and suffering from asthma and honest respiratory diseases.

Early swimming helps develop the baby's muscles and contributes to his rapid physical development. Water reduces the increased muscle tone that is inherent in newborns in the first months of their life and thus accelerates the development of their motor skills. Physical movements in a water environment strengthen the muscles of the abdomen, back, neck, arms and legs.

The water caresses the baby's skin with optimal and very effective pressure - this improves blood circulation in the body and strengthens the child's fragile nervous system, respectively. During immersion under water and the accompanying holding of breath, blood circulation to the brain is stimulated and it is saturated with oxygen. This, in turn, leads to its rapid development.

Children who start swimming before and at preschool age give a proper vector of growth. Thus, swimming supports the development of balance and coordination in babies.

Swimming has been proven to stimulate the digestive system, which leads to regular eating. It is also an excellent prevention of diseases such as obesity and chronic overweight.

Water provides new sensory stimuli for babies. This helps the development of their tactile, visual and auditory perception.

Stimulating blood circulation develops the heart rhythm by improving the work of the heart.

For babies and children up to 2 years of age, wearing a hat is optional. We also clothe children in disposable or reusable waterproof diapers.

Swimming has a calming effect on babies, helping to deal with stress and tension. A calm, happy and relaxed baby is a guarantee of a good and quality sleep!

Babies can learn basic water safety skills such as backstroke and belly swimming.

Regular swimming from early childhood and excellent prevention against frequent illnesses and diseases of the respiratory tract and respiratory system. Prevents diseases such as scoliosis, stooping, posture disorders and muscle dystonia.

The development of discipline, self-control, self-control, courage and determination, as well as the ability to make decisions in difficult situations are undeniable long-term results of early childhood swimming. Psychological research shows that when diving deep underwater and overcoming the resistance of the water column, the child develops qualities such as purposefulness, sociability, independence and leadership qualities.
